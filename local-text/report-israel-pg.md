# Paul Graham on Israel/Palestine: Year-by-Year Analysis (2004-2026)

Merged from three source sets: (1) curated commentary list with essays, HN comments, tweets, quote tweets, and replies; (2) earlier Grok X API extraction; (3) press archive research (Globes, Times of Israel, Fox Business, Scott Aaronson, Moguldom).

## Taxonomy of posts

To separate PG's own stated positions from content he amplifies, every entry is classified:

- **[essay]** Paragraph or sentence within a paulgraham.com essay
- **[HN]** First-person Hacker News comment by user "pg"
- **[1P]** First-person tweet: PG's own words asserting something
- **[reply]** First-person reply to another user
- **[QT]** Quote tweet: PG's words framing another user's post
- **[LINK]** Article share with article title, no documented first-person commentary

## Year-by-year

### 2004 - first preserved mention (essay)

- [essay] [Jan 2004 "What You Can't Say"](https://paulgraham.com/say.html) - in a discussion of moral fashion and what cannot be said, mentions that pro-Israel groups compile dossiers on people who speak out against Israeli human rights abuses

This is the earliest preserved PG reference to the Israel debate. The framing positions Israeli critics as a category subject to organized pressure.

### 2006 (essay)

- [essay] [May 2006 "How to Be Silicon Valley"](https://paulgraham.com/america.html) - notes that building a silicon valley in Israel would only attract Jews given their small global population share

Demographic/structural observation, not a position on the conflict.

### 2007 (essay)

- [essay] [Oct 2007 "The Future of Web Startups"](https://paulgraham.com/startuphubs.html) - uses Jews telling Jewish jokes as an analogy for who has standing to make in-group criticism

Cultural framing, not conflict-related.

### 2009 - first first-person statement

- [HN] [Jan 3, 2009](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=418512) - "Following the sentence 'no politics' with one equating criticism of Israel with anti-semitism is a bit hypocritical."

First preserved first-person position. The substantive view PG reiterated in his Dec 10, 2023 tweet was already on record 14 years earlier.

## Observed shifts across the 22-year record

**Mode of engagement shifts over time:**

1. **2004-2014: essays and article shares only.** Position visible in 1 essay sentence (2004) and 11 article shares. The 2009 HN comment is the only first-person assertion in this period.
2. **2021: first first-person tweet** ("the one million children who live there are the primary victims").
3. **2023 Q4: full mode-of-engagement diversification.** First-person, quote tweets, replies, and article shares all active simultaneously. The Dec 10 first-person statement on the Israel/Jews distinction is his most policy-explicit declaration.
4. **2024: argumentative mode.** Heavy first-person volume; engagement with critics; direct intent attribution to Israeli decision-makers (Apr 4 Lavender tweet).
5. **2025: reply-heavy.** ~50% of posts are replies engaging with other users, often defending Palestinians from specific accusations or pushing back on hasbara/fake-account allegations. First first-person use of "genocide" on Sep 1, 2025.
6. **2026: framework-level.** Netanyahu grouped with Putin and Trump as a class of autocratic wartime leaders. Iran-Israel dimension appears.

**Substantive positions on record:**

- **Israel-criticism vs antisemitism distinction**: present in [2009 HN comment](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=418512), restated [Dec 10, 2023](https://x.com/paulg/status/1733833508122992878) and [Feb 16, 2024](https://x.com/paulg/status/1758580760494088282). Identical position across 16 years.
- **October 7 acknowledgment**: present in [Nov 6, 2023 reply](https://x.com/paulg/status/1721481102848602416) ("1400 Israelis were murdered and 240 taken hostage on October 7; no reasonable person denies that").
- **Netanyahu-specific criticism**: extensive. Office-clinging motive ([Mar 20, 2024](https://x.com/paulg/status/1770398325545591152)), war-prolonging ([Jul 11, 2025](https://x.com/paulg/status/1943706649291600321)), peace-rejection ([Aug 10, 2025](https://x.com/paulg/status/1954585428558073947)), autocrat-class membership ([Apr 15, 2026](https://x.com/paulg/status/2044341837788217730)).
- **Distinction between Netanyahu and Israelis generally**: ["Many Israelis disagree with Netanyahu's policies"](https://x.com/paulg/status/1924538885465358538) (May 19, 2025).
- **"Genocide" framing**: first amplified via Israeli sources ([Jul 28, 2025](https://x.com/paulg/status/1949952550557384964) Benyair), then adopted first-person ([Sep 1, 2025](https://x.com/paulg/status/1962512489452618056) "It's official. It's genocide").
- **Free speech / academic-suppression criticism**: ["didn't speak out against both woke censorship and Trump's attempts to suppress criticism of Israel"](https://x.com/paulg/status/1901940573499498875) (Mar 18, 2025); shadowban defenses for de Icaza ([Feb 21](https://x.com/paulg/status/1760352385484865778), [Mar 14, 2024](https://x.com/paulg/status/1768339827592777975)).
